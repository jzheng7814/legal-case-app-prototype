BREACH OF CONTRACT COMPLAINT

Plaintiff Sarah Johnson, through her counsel, respectfully alleges:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff Sarah Johnson is a sole proprietor operating a technology consulting business in San Francisco, California.

2. Defendant XYZ Corporation is a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business in Palo Alto, California, engaged in software development and licensing.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

3. On January 10, 2024, the parties entered into a Software Licensing Agreement ("Agreement") whereby Defendant agreed to provide comprehensive software solutions and technical support services to Plaintiff's business operations.

4. The Agreement specifically required Defendant to provide quarterly software updates, 24/7 technical support, and on-site assistance as detailed in Section 4.2 of the contract.

5. Plaintiff paid $75,000 upfront for these services with the reasonable expectation of continuous, reliable support throughout the contract term.

BREACH OF CONTRACT

6. Defendant materially breached the Agreement by:
   a) Failing to provide the Q1 2024 software update by the agreed deadline of April 1, 2024
   b) Consistently exceeding the contracted 24-hour response time for technical support
   c) Refusing to provide promised on-site technical assistance despite multiple formal requests
   d) Allowing software to become incompatible with industry-standard operating systems

7. These breaches caused substantial harm to Plaintiff's business operations, resulting in lost clients and revenue.

DEFENDANT'S INADEQUATE RESPONSE

8. When confronted about these failures, Defendant offered only excuses including alleged security vulnerabilities, staffing issues, and COVID-related restrictions.

9. While some technical challenges may have been legitimate, Defendant failed to communicate proactively or provide adequate interim solutions as required under the Agreement.

DAMAGES

10. As a direct result of Defendant's material breach, Plaintiff has suffered:
    - Direct costs of $20,000 in emergency technical solutions
    - Lost business revenue of $25,000 from client departures
    - Damage to business reputation and ongoing client relationships

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests:
A. Judgment against Defendant in the amount of $45,000 in actual damages
B. Pre and post-judgment interest
C. Costs and reasonable attorney's fees
D. Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper
